how it works — what the camera sees & how it shapes the poem ▼
face signals
close to cam your face fills most of the frame : the poem gets up-close and personal, almost uncomfortably so
far from cam you're small in the frame : the poem goes soft and distant, like a memory fading out
bright eyes your eyes reflect a lot of light : the poem reads you as switched on, alert, paying attention
dim eyes low reflected light : the poem goes quieter, more still, like you're resting or zoning out
eyes wide apart the distance between your eyes looks wide relative to your face : the poem opens up, expansive
eyes close together eyes appear closer to center : the poem narrows, concentrates, pulls inward
mouth open detected gap between lips : the poem picks up on speech, breath, something unsaid
mouth closed lips together : the poem treats it as held-back, sealed, keeping something in
head tilted left ear angled toward left shoulder : the poem leans into doubt, a sideways kind of thinking
head tilted right ear toward right shoulder : the poem reads it as listening, leaning into something
hand signals
one hand visible a single hand enters frame : the poem notices it like a new clause in a sentence, something added
two hands visible both hands in frame : the poem treats this as a conversation happening between them
hand open / spread fingertips spread far from the wrist : open palm, open gesture, the poem reads offering or stopping
hand closed / fist fingers curl close to wrist : the poem goes tighter, more private, something held back
hand raised high wrist is in the upper half of frame : the poem reaches upward, signals, lifts
hand held low wrist in lower half of frame : the poem drops down, settles, comes to rest
no hands nothing detected in the frame : the poem falls back on your face alone, or reaches for something universal
